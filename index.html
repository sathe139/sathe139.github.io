Marsha P. Johnson

Previous Next
"How many years has it taken people to realize that we are all brothers and sisters and human beings in the human race?"
  • 1945 Born in Elizabeth, New Jersey as Malcolm Michaels Jr.

  • 1963 Graduated from the former Edison High School and left for New York City

  • 1966 Legally changed her name to Marsha P. Johnson and moved to Greenwich Village permanently. She lived on the streets as a sex worker and was arrested many times.

  • 1969 The Stonewall uprising occurred (It is unclear whether Johnson helped start the uprising or was even present at the time, but many people recall seeing her there resisting the police)

  • 1970 Joined the Gay Liberation Front and participated in the first Christopher Street Liberation Pride rally on the first anniversary of the Stonewall rebellion. She staged a sit- in protest with other GLF members at Weinstein Hall after an NYU dance was cancelled due to being sponsored by gay organizations. She later co-founded the Street Transvestite Action Revolutionaries (STAR) organization with Sylvia Rivera.

  • 1972 Performed as a member of Hot Peaches, a drag performance troupe from NYC, into the 1990s and established the STAR House, a shelter for homeless LGBT youth, with Sylvia Rivera

  • 1973 Johnson, Rivera, and all drag queens were banned from participating in the gay pride parade by the commity running the event. In response they marched ahead of the parade.

  • 1975 Posed for Andy Warhol’s series titled “Ladies and Gentlemen”

  • 1980 Continued her street activism as an organizer and marshal with ACT UP through the 1980s

  • 1990 Performed in London with the Hot Peaches as an AIDS activist

  • 1992 Passed away at the age of 46. Her body was found in the Hudson River. Johnson’s death was ruled a suicide despite the claims that she was not suicidal by those close to her. Witnesses spoke of seeing Johnson being harassed earlier that day.
